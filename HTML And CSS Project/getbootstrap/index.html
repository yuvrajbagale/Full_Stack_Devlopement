Comprehension Check (p. 235)

Q.: Into what kind of creature has Gregor Samsa been transformed?
A.: He has been changed into a bug.

Comprehension Check (p. 237)

Q.: What does Gregor do for a living?
A.: He is a traveling salesman.

Comprehension Check (p. 241)

Q.: Why does the manager come to Gregor’s home?
A.: The manager comes to see why Gregor has not yet left for work.

Comprehension Check (p. 243)

Q.: Why does Gregor’s family get upset when he speaks?
A.: They get upset because he sounds like an animal and they can’t understand what he is saying.

Comprehension Check (p. 247)

Q.: What causes Gregor to feel physically well for the first time that day?
A.: Gregor lands on his legs like an insect instead of trying to stand upright like a human.

Comprehension Check (p. 251)

Q.: Where does Gregor sleep at night?
A.: He sleeps underneath the couch.

Comprehension Check (p. 255)

Q.: Why does Gregor’s sister push the chair near the window when she finishes cleaning Gregor’s room?
A.: She notices that Gregor himself has moved the chair near the window, so she makes sure to move it back out of kindness.

Comprehension Check (p. 259)

Q.: Why does Gregor at first welcome the removal of the furniture in his room but then change his mind about it?
A.: At first, Gregor welcomes the removal of the furniture because an empty space would present him with few obstacles. He changes his mind when he realizes that the removal of the furniture means he is abandoning his humanity. 

Comprehension Check (p. 263)

Q.: How does Gregor get injured?
A.: His father throws apples at him.

Comprehension Check (p. 269)

Q.: Why does Gregor enter the living room?
A.: His sister begins playing the violin.

Comprehension Check (p. 273)

Q.: After the lodgers announce their departure, what does Grete say the family must do?
A.: Grete says they must stop thinking of the “animal” as Gregor. Instead of caring for him, they must get rid of him and move on with their lives.
